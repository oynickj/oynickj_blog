Hey! Thanks for stopping by. I'm Josh and this is my personal page. I'm a developer, borough adventurer, lifelong student, and believer that the most important things in life are to work hard and be kind. I was born and raised in Sarasota, Florida, and my parents are both from Mexico City, so I grew up speaking Spanish and eating Mexican Jewish food.

My journey as a developer began in the fall of 2013, when I enrolled in General Assembly's Web Development Immersive program.

Before that, I was an investment banking analyst at UBS and Greentech Capital Advisors, where I worked with companies in the renewable energy and infrastructure sectors.

I graduated from the University of Pennsylvania's Wharton School in 2011 with concentrations in Finance and Operations and Information Management. At Penn, I was in a musical comedy group called the Mask and Wig Club where I performed in the cast and played drums, guitar, keys, and sometimes the shaker in the band.

Granulr is group project created during General Assembly's Web Development Immersive program. Built with Javascript, Ruby, and Rails, Granulr facilitates goal setting and achievement through a "user stories" model and allows users to break down large goals into specific, actionable tasks. I'm currently using Granulr to help me as I train for my first half marathon in March 2014.

Getadilla, my first project, is an online ordering portal for a quesadilla restaurant. Inspired by my love for the world's greatest food, the app is built with Rails and incorporates login and authentication, as well as Google Translate functionality.

This project was my first foray into the awesome worlds of the Foursquare API and the D3.js JavaScript library. I love traveling and finding cool places to visit, so I wanted an app that allows users to find out which venues have the most checkins based on a city and category of their choice.
